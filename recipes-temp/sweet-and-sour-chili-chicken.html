Sweet and Sour Chili Chicken

Seafood-free • cilantro-free • extra crunch • pineapple-forward glossy glaze

← Back to Home
Prep: 15 min Marinate: 15 min Cook: 12–15 min Total: ~45 min Serves: 4

Ingredients

1) The Chicken & Marinade (Velveting)

  • 1.5 lbs chicken breast or thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil

Mix and let sit for 15 minutes.

2) The “Gold Standard” Sauce

Whisk together in a bowl:

  • 3 tbsp sweet chili sauce (or substitute below)
  • 4 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce (seafood-safe)
  • 2 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • 2 tbsp ketchup
  • 2 tbsp pineapple juice (reserved from the can)
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar

Sweet Chili Substitute (Quick)

  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes

3) The Fresh Lineup

  • Produce: 2 bell peppers (sliced), 1 cup snap peas, 1 head baby bok choi (chopped)
  • The Crunch: 1–2 cups fresh bean sprouts
  • The Fruit: 1 can (~14 oz) pineapple chunks or tidbits, drained (reserve 2 tbsp juice)
  • Aromatics: 2 cloves garlic (minced) + 1 tbsp fresh ginger (grated)
  • Finishers: 1 tsp toasted sesame oil + fresh lime

Instructions

  1. The Sear
    Heat 2 tbsp oil in a wok or skillet over high heat. Add chicken in a single layer. Sear 3 minutes undisturbed, flip, and cook 2 more minutes. Remove chicken to a plate.
  2. The Fruit & Veg
    In the same pan, add pineapple chunks, peppers, and snap peas. Stir-fry 3 minutes. The pineapple will start to caramelize at the edges.
  3. The Aromatics
    Add garlic and ginger; stir-fry 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. The Glaze
    Pour in sauce mixture. Let it bubble 1 minute until it thickens into a glossy glaze.
  5. The Final Mix
    Add chicken back in with baby bok choi and bean sprouts. Toss 60–90 seconds until sprouts are just tender but still “snappy.”
  6. Off-Heat
    Turn off heat. Stir in toasted sesame oil and a squeeze of lime.

Notes

  • If the sauce gets too thick, loosen with a splash of water or broth.
  • Serve over rice, noodles, or cauliflower rice.
