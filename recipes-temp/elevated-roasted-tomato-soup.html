Elevated Roasted Tomato Soup

4 garlic cloves • balsamic roast • caramelized paste • butter & milk finish

← Back to Home
Prep: 10 min Roast: 45 min Simmer: 10 min Total: ~65 min Serves: 4–6

Ingredients

The Roast

  • 3 lbs ripe tomatoes (halved if small/medium, quartered if large)
  • 1 large yellow onion (quartered and separated)
  • 4 garlic cloves (peeled and trimmed)
  • 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1–2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt (plus more to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp fresh ground black pepper

The Soup Base

  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken bone broth (or low-sodium stock)
  • 2 tsp sugar (plus more to taste)

The Finish

  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter (cold, cut into small cubes)
  • 4 tbsp fresh basil, chopped

Instructions

  1. The Balsamic Roast
    Preheat oven to 400°F. On a large sheet pan, toss tomatoes, onion, and garlic with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, salt, and pepper. Roast 40–45 minutes until tomatoes are blistered and onions have some charred edges.
  2. Caramelize the Paste
    While vegetables roast, place a large pot over medium heat. Add a tiny splash of oil and the tomato paste. Stir constantly 2–3 minutes until it turns a deep, dark brick-red. This removes the “tinny” taste and builds a rich savory base.
  3. Simmer
    Transfer roasted vegetables and all pan juices into the pot. Add broth and sugar. Scrape the bottom of the pot to release the caramelized paste. Simmer gently for 10 minutes.
  4. Blend
    Use an immersion blender to blend until completely smooth. (If using a standard blender, work in batches and vent the lid.)
  5. The Silk Finish
    Turn heat to low. Stir in the milk. Once incorporated, whisk in cold butter cubes one by one. Whisking cold butter into warm soup creates a velvety emulsion and a professional sheen. Stir in basil last.

Notes

  • Start with 1 tbsp balsamic; add the second tbsp if your tomatoes need more brightness.
  • If the soup tastes too sharp, add sugar 1/2 tsp at a time until balanced.
  • For extra richness, swap part of the milk for cream.
