Beef Stroganoff

Cream of mushroom base • Better Than Bouillon boost • milk/butter swap for glossy sauce

← Back to Home
Prep: 10 min Cook: 20 min Total: 30 min Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • Beef: 1 lb sirloin strips (or lean ground beef)
  • Soup base: 1 can (10.5 oz) condensed cream of mushroom soup
  • Boost: 1/2 tsp Better Than Bouillon (beef base)
  • Milk: 3/8 cup (6 tbsp)
  • Butter: 2 tbsp (plus 1 tbsp for searing)
  • Dijon mustard: 1 tsp
  • Worcestershire sauce: 1 tbsp
  • Spices: 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp paprika, 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • Base: 8–12 oz egg noodles, cooked and drained

Instructions

  1. Sear the Beef
    In a large skillet over medium-high heat, melt 1 tbsp butter (or use a little oil). Add the beef and cook until browned (strips: ~2 minutes per side; ground beef: until no longer pink).
  2. Rest the Meat
    Transfer the beef (and any juices!) to a plate or bowl. Cover loosely with foil to keep it warm while you make the sauce.
  3. Build the Sauce Base
    In the same skillet (don’t wash it), reduce heat to medium. Add cream of mushroom soup, Better Than Bouillon, Worcestershire, and Dijon mustard. Whisk until the bouillon dissolves.
  4. Simmer with Spices
    Stir in garlic powder, paprika, and black pepper.
  5. Add Creaminess
    Pour in the milk and add the 2 tbsp butter. Stir continuously for 3–4 minutes until melted and the sauce is smooth, glossy, and gently simmering.
  6. The Grand Reunion
    Turn heat to low. Add the beef (plus resting juices) and cooked egg noodles back into the skillet.
  7. Toss and Heat
    Toss gently for 1–2 minutes until the beef is reheated and noodles are fully coated (“drenched”). Serve hot.

Notes

  • If the sauce gets too thick, splash in a tablespoon or two of noodle water or broth.
  • For extra tang, add an extra 1/2 tsp Dijon at the end.
