Beef & Broccoli Noodles

Tender velveted beef, bright broccoli, and glossy sauce tossed with egg noodles.

← Back to Home
Prep: 20 min Cook: 10 min Total: 30 min Serves: 2–3

Ingredients

The Protein & Veggies

  • 450g flank steak or sirloin, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 2 large heads broccoli, cut into bite-sized florets
  • 250g–280g egg noodles, cooked al dente
  • Aromatics: 3 cloves garlic (minced), 1 tsp fresh ginger (grated)

The Marinade (Velveting)

  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce

The Pro-Blend Sauce

  • 180ml beef broth
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1.5 tsp cornstarch

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Beef
    In a bowl, toss the sliced beef with the cornstarch and soy sauce. Let it sit at room temperature for 15–20 minutes. This “velveting” technique protects the meat from the high heat, keeping it tender.
  2. Prepare the Broccoli & Noodles
    Broccoli: Place florets in a microwave-safe bowl with a splash of water. Cover and microwave for 2 minutes until bright green. Drain and set aside.
    Noodles: Boil egg noodles, draining them 1 minute earlier than the package suggests. Rinse with cold water and toss with a drop of oil to prevent sticking.
  3. Sear the Beef
    Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat. Add the beef in a single layer. Let it sear undisturbed for 2 minutes to get a dark crust, then flip and cook for 1 more minute. Remove beef from the pan and set aside.
  4. Build the Sauce
    Add a tiny splash of oil to the pan if dry. Sauté the garlic and ginger for 30 seconds. In a small bowl, whisk together the Pro-Blend Sauce ingredients, then pour into the pan. Stir constantly for 1–2 minutes until the liquid thickens into a glossy, dark glaze.
  5. The Final Toss
    Add the beef, broccoli, and noodles into the pan. Toss vigorously over high heat for about 1 minute. The noodles will finish cooking as they soak up the sauce.

Pro Tips

  • Uniformity: Slice the beef to a consistent 3mm–5mm thickness so it cooks evenly.
  • The Glaze: If the sauce becomes too thick, add a tablespoon of water or broth to loosen it up.
