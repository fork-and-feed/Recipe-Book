Classic Oatmeal Cookies

Soft-centered, lightly chewy oatmeal cookies with a golden edge—classic and endlessly customizable.

← Back to Home
Yield: ~48 cookies Prep: 25 min Oven: 375°F Bake: 8–10 min

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup butter, softened
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves (optional)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups rolled oats

Instructions

  1. Cream the Base
    In a large mixing bowl, beat butter with an electric mixer on medium-high speed for 30 seconds.
  2. Mix Sugars & Leavening
    Add brown sugar, granulated sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and (if using) cinnamon and cloves. Beat until combined, scraping the bowl occasionally.
  3. Add Wet Ingredients
    Beat in eggs and vanilla until combined.
  4. Incorporate Flour
    Beat in as much flour as you can with the mixer. Stir in remaining flour by hand.
  5. Add Oats
    Stir in rolled oats.
  6. Portion & Bake
    Drop dough by rounded teaspoons, 2 inches apart, onto an ungreased cookie sheet. Bake at 375°F for 8–10 minutes, or until edges are golden.
  7. Cool
    Cool on the cookie sheet for 1 minute, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

Variations

  • Oatmeal-Raisin: Stir in 1 cup raisins or dried tart cherries after the oats. (Makes ~54)
  • Oatmeal-Chip: Stir in 1 cup chocolate, butterscotch, or peanut butter chips and 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans. (Makes ~54)
  • Oversize Cookies: Use a 1/4-cup measure to drop mounds 2 inches apart. Press into 3-inch circles. Bake 8–10 minutes. (Makes 10)

Quick Tip

  • For the best texture, use butter that’s softened (pliable, not melted) and pack brown sugar firmly in the cup.
