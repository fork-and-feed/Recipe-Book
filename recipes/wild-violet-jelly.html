Wild Violet Jelly

Bright, floral jelly made from wild violet “tea” with lemon and pectin.

← Back to Home
Prep: overnight Cook: 15 min Yield: ~5–6 half-pints

Ingredients

  • Wild violet petals: 1-liter mason jar, half-filled (approx. 500 ml of petals)
  • Boiling water: enough to fill the jar
  • Lemon juice: 1/4 cup
  • Sugar: 4 cups
  • Pectin: 1 box (approx. 49–50 g)

Instructions

  1. Steep: Place wild violet petals in a 1-liter jar (fill about halfway). Pour boiling water over the petals until the jar is full. Cover and let steep overnight.
  2. Strain: Drain the liquid through a fine strainer or cheesecloth, squeezing the petals to extract all the color and flavor.
  3. Mix: In a large pot, combine the violet liquid, 1/4 cup lemon juice, and the pectin. (The lemon juice reacts with the violet tea to create a bright purple color.)
  4. First boil: Bring the mixture to a hard boil.
  5. Add sugar: Stir in the sugar until fully dissolved.
  6. Final boil: Bring back to a boil and boil vigorously for 2 minutes, stirring frequently.
  7. Can: Pour into hot jars immediately. Process in a boiling-water canning bath according to standard canning safety guidelines.

Notes

  • Use only petals from violets you’re confident are edible and pesticide-free.
  • For best set, use the pectin type/box size your recipe was written for and follow the boil timing closely.
