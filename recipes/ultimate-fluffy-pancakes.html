The Ultimate Fluffy Pancakes

Thick, tender pancakes with the “don’t overmix” method and a short batter rest.

← Back to Home
Prep: 10 min Rest: 5–10 min Cook: 10 min Total: ~25 min Serves: 3–4

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 tbsp melted butter (cooled slightly)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Whisk Dry Ingredients
    In a large bowl, sift together flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt.
  2. Mix Wet Ingredients
    In a separate bowl, whisk egg, milk, vanilla, and melted butter.
  3. Combine
    Pour wet into dry. Stir with a spatula until just combined. Leave lumps — they’re the secret to fluff!
  4. The Rest
    Let batter sit undisturbed for 5–10 minutes so the baking powder can activate.
  5. Cook
    Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-low with a little butter or oil. Scoop 1/4 cup batter per pancake. Don’t stir the batter again before scooping.
  6. The Flip
    When bubbles form and edges look set/matte, flip gently. Cook 1–2 minutes more until golden brown.

3 Secrets for Success

  • Don’t overmix: Stop stirring while small lumps remain to keep pancakes tender.
  • Don’t press: Never press down after flipping — it ruins the air pockets.
  • Heat control: Medium-low cooks the thick batter through without burning the outside.
