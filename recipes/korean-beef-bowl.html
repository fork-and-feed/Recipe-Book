Korean Beef Bowl

Broth-infused rice, sweet-salty glazed beef, and quick tangy cucumber pickles.

← Back to Home
Prep: 10 min Cook: 20 min Total: 30 min Serves: 4

Ingredients

The Savory Rice

  • 1 cup jasmine or basmati rice
  • 1.5–2 cups chicken or beef broth (use amount suggested on your rice package)
  • Optional: 1 clove smashed garlic or a slice of ginger tossed into the pot

The Quick Pickles

  • 2 mini cucumbers (Persian), thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp sesame oil
  • Pinch of salt

The Beef & Sauce

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1/2 Asian pear (or sweet apple), finely grated
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
  • 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes

Instructions

  1. Simmer the broth rice: Rinse the rice until the water runs clear. Add it to a pot with broth (instead of water). Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer according to package directions.
  2. Prep the pickles: Toss sliced cucumbers with rice vinegar, sugar, salt, and 1/2 tsp sesame oil. Let marinate while everything cooks.
  3. Toast the aromatics: Heat 1 tbsp sesame oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add minced garlic and ginger. Sauté 1–2 minutes until fragrant and lightly golden.
  4. Brown & caramelize: Add ground beef. Brown thoroughly and drain excess fat. In a small bowl, whisk grated pear/apple with soy sauce, brown sugar, and red pepper flakes. Pour over beef and simmer 3–5 minutes until glossy and slightly thickened.
  5. Assemble: Fluff the broth-infused rice, top with glazed beef, and finish with a pile of pickled cucumbers.

The Flavor Profile

  • The rice: Deep, savory, and rich.
  • The beef: Sweet, salty, and tender with fruit-driven caramelization.
  • The pickles: Cold, crisp, and acidic to cleanse the palate.
