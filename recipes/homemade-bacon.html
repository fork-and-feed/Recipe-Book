Homemade Bacon Recipe

Simple cured bacon using weight-based ratios + Cure #1, then smoke and cook to temp.

← Back to Home
Cure: 7 days Dry: 24 hrs Smoke: 1 hr Cook: to 145°F Rest: 24 hrs

Ingredients

  • Pork belly: base weight (weigh your meat)
  • Salt: 2.5% of pork belly weight
  • Sugar: 5.0% of pork belly weight (maple syrup, or 50/50 syrup + brown sugar)
  • Curing Salt #1 (Prague Powder): 25g per 10kg of meat (2.5g per 1kg)

Instructions

  1. Cure
    Combine cure ingredients with the pork belly. Seal (vacuum seal or ziplock) and refrigerate for 7 days.
  2. Dry
    Remove from packaging and let air dry in the refrigerator for 24 hours.
  3. Cold Smoke
    Cold smoke the belly for 1 hour.
  4. Cook
    Raise smoker/oven temperature to 170°F (77°C). Hold until the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C).
  5. If needed
    If internal temp isn’t reached after 5 hours, increase smoker/oven temp to 180°F (82°C).
  6. Remove
    Take the bacon out once it reaches a maximum internal temperature of 145°F (63°C).
  7. Rest
    Wrap and cool in the fridge for 24 hours before slicing.

Quick Math Guide (per 1kg of meat)

  • Salt: 25g
  • Sugar/Syrup: 50g
  • Curing Salt #1: 2.5g

Notes

  • Use a scale for accurate curing ratios.
  • Slice after the 24-hour rest for cleaner, thinner slices.
