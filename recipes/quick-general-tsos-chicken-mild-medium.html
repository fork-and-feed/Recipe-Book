Quick General Tso’s Chicken (Mild & Medium)

Air-fried popcorn chicken tossed in a fast, glossy General Tso’s-style sauce — choose mild or medium heat.

← Back to Home
Prep: 2 min Cook: 15 min Total: 17 min Serves: 2–4

Ingredients

The Chicken

  • 1 bag (approx. 700 g) Prime breaded popcorn chicken

The Slurry (Thickener)

  • 1 tbsp cornstarch + 1 tbsp cold water

The Sauce Base

  • 1/2 cup chicken broth (or water)
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp fresh ginger, grated

Choose Your Heat Level

  • Mild: add 3 tbsp sugar or honey
  • Medium: add 1 1/2 tbsp sugar or honey and 1–2 tsp Lee Kum Kee (LKK) Chiu Chow Chili Oil

Quick Method

  1. Air fry: Cook popcorn chicken at 400°F (200°C) for 12–14 minutes. (Air fryer is best — it keeps the breading from getting soggy once sauced.)
  2. Simmer: In a large skillet over medium heat, whisk together the Sauce Base plus your chosen heat-level ingredients. Bring to a bubble and simmer for 2 minutes.
  3. Thicken: Stir the cornstarch slurry, then pour it into the pan while whisking. Cook about 45 seconds until thick and glossy.
  4. Toss: Turn off heat. Add the hot chicken and toss for about 15 seconds until coated. Serve immediately over rice.

Pro-Tip: Split Batch for a Family

Make the Mild version first. Remove about half the sauce and chicken. Then stir 1 tsp LKK chili oil into the remaining sauce, simmer for 10 seconds, and toss the rest of the chicken for the Medium version.
