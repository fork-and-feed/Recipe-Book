Creamy Chicken Tetrazzini

Creamy baked pasta with chicken and your choice of mushrooms or peas (adapted from Natasha’s Kitchen).

← Back to Home
Prep: 20 min Cook: 35 min Total: ~55 min Pan: 9×13

Ingredients

The Base

  • 12 oz linguine or spaghetti (bronze-cut/durum wheat preferred)
  • 4 cups cooked chicken, shredded or diced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 1/2 cups mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped

Vegetable Choice (pick one)

  • Mushroom version: 1 lb button mushrooms, sliced thick
  • Pea version: 1 1/2 cups frozen peas (no need to thaw)

The Sauce (choose one version)

  • Option A (half-and-half): 4 tbsp (57 g) unsalted butter + 2 cups half-and-half
  • Option B (milk substitute): 8 tbsp (113 g) unsalted butter + 1 3/4 cups whole milk

Shared Sauce Ingredients

  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 1/2 tsp sea salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper

Instructions

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 350°F. Butter a 9×13-inch baking dish.
  2. Pasta: Boil pasta in salted water until barely al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water, then drain and set pasta aside.
  3. Veggies: Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large skillet.
    • If using mushrooms: sauté mushrooms for 3 minutes, then add onions and cook until golden (5–7 minutes). Add garlic for the last 1 minute.
    • If using peas: sauté only the onions until golden (5–7 minutes). Add garlic for the last 1 minute. (You’ll add the frozen peas later.)
  4. The sauce: In the same skillet, melt your butter: 4 tbsp for Option A or 8 tbsp for Option B. Whisk in 1/3 cup flour for about 1 1/2 minutes until lightly golden. Slowly whisk in chicken broth, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Whisk in your dairy (half-and-half or milk) and bring to a simmer until smooth.
  5. Combine: In the large pasta pot, mix noodles, chicken, sautéed onion/garlic, and the sauce. Stir in the 1 1/2 cups frozen peas now (if using). Add a splash of reserved pasta water if it needs more creaminess.
  6. Bake: Transfer to the baking dish and top with mozzarella. Bake covered with foil for 20 minutes. Remove foil and bake uncovered for 15 minutes. Sprinkle with parsley to finish.

Notes

  • If your skillet gets too dry during sauce-making, whisk in a splash of broth to smooth it out.
  • Let the casserole rest 5–10 minutes before serving so it sets up.
