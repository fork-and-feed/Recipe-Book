Spicy Beef Lo Mein

Marinated, tender beef + spicy-glossy sauce + quick stir-fried veggies.

← Back to Home
Serves: 2–3 Prep: 10 min Cook: 10 min Total: 20 min

Ingredients (2–3 servings)

Noodles + Oil

  • 225 g lo mein noodles
  • 2–3 tbsp neutral oil (for stir-frying)

Marinated Beef

  • 1/2 lb beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 2 tsp light soy sauce
  • 3/4 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tsp chili crisp oil

Sauce

  • 3 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 2 tsp light soy sauce
  • 2 tsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 tsp beef bouillon paste

Aromatics + Vegetables

  • 1 shallot, diced
  • Thai chilies, to taste
  • 1/2 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 handful mushrooms, sliced
  • Scallions, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Cook noodles & prep beef: Boil noodles until slightly underdone, then drain well. Slice beef thinly against the grain.
  2. Marinate beef: Toss beef with light soy sauce, sugar, baking soda, chili crisp, and cornstarch until evenly coated and slightly sticky.
  3. Make sauce: Mix oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and beef bouillon paste in a small bowl. Set aside.
  4. Sear beef: Heat 1–2 tbsp oil in a wok or large pan over high heat. Add beef in a single layer and sear until browned. Remove and set aside.
  5. Stir-fry vegetables: Add a bit more oil if needed. Cook shallots and chilies about 1 minute, then add carrots, bell pepper, and mushrooms. Stir-fry until slightly softened but still crisp.
  6. Combine: Add noodles and sauce and toss until well coated. Return beef to the pan and toss thoroughly to heat through. Finish with scallions.

Notes

  • Slightly undercook noodles so they don’t get mushy when stir-frying.
  • Baking soda + cornstarch helps keep the beef tender (velveting technique).
