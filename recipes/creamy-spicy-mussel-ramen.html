Creamy Spicy Mussel Ramen

Spicy ramen turned creamy and glossy — topped with plump mussels and a hit of lime.

← Back to Home
Prep: 5 min Cook: 12 min Total: 17 min Serves: 1

Ingredients

The Base

  • 1 pack spicy ramen (Shin Ramyun is the gold standard)

The Star

  • 1 lb mussels (fresh & scrubbed, or frozen)

The Liquids

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup whole milk

The Aromatics

  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • 1 green onion, sliced (keep whites and greens separate)

The Richness

  • 2 tbsp butter (1 tbsp for sautéing, 1 tbsp for finishing)

The Pop

  • 1/2 lime (essential to cut the creaminess)

Instructions

  1. Sauté: Melt 1 tbsp butter in a pot over medium heat. Add garlic, ginger, and the whites of the green onion. Sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
  2. Simmer base: Add the ramen seasoning packet, 1 cup water, and 1 cup milk. Bring to a gentle simmer (avoid a rolling boil so the milk doesn’t separate).
  3. Steam mussels: Add mussels to the simmering liquid and cover with a lid.
    • Fresh: Steam 3–5 minutes until they pop open.
    • Frozen: Simmer 2–3 minutes until hot.
    Remove mussels with tongs and set aside so they stay plump.
  4. Cook noodles: Add noodles to the creamy broth and cook for the package time, stirring frequently so the milk doesn’t scorch.
  5. The finish: Turn off the heat. Stir in the final 1 tbsp butter and a squeeze of lime.
  6. Serve: Pour noodles and broth into a bowl, top with mussels and green onion tops.

Pro Tips

  • Milk secret: Half milk/half water gives a rich “chowder” consistency without being as heavy as pure cream.
  • Spice level: Milk mellows heat. For extra hot, add 1 tsp gochujang or chili oil at the end.
  • Butter: Stirring in the final cold pat off-heat gives a glossy, restaurant-style finish.
