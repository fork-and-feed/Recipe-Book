Dandelion Jelly

Sunny, honey-like jelly made from dandelion “tea” with lemon and pectin.

← Back to Home
Prep: overnight Cook: 15 min Yield: ~5–6 half-pints

Ingredients

  • Dandelion petals: 1-liter mason jar, loosely filled (yellow petals only)
  • Boiling water: enough to fill the jar
  • Lemon juice: 3 tbsp
  • Sugar: 4 cups
  • Pectin: 1 box (approx. 49–50 g)

Instructions

  1. Steep: Fill a 1-liter jar with dandelion petals. Pour boiling water over the petals until the jar is full. Cover and let steep overnight.
  2. Strain: Drain through a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth, squeezing the petals well to extract all the liquid.
  3. Mix: In a large pot, combine the dandelion liquid, lemon juice, and pectin.
  4. First boil: Bring the mixture to a hard boil.
  5. Add sugar: Stir in the sugar until fully dissolved.
  6. Final boil: Bring back to a boil and boil vigorously for 2 minutes, stirring frequently.
  7. Can: Pour into hot jars immediately. Process in a boiling-water canning bath according to standard canning safety guidelines.

Notes

  • Use only clean, pesticide-free dandelions and petals-only (remove all green parts to avoid bitterness).
  • For best set, match the pectin type/box size your recipe calls for and keep the full rolling boil timing.
