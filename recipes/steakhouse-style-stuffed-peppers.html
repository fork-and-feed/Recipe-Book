Steakhouse-Style Stuffed Peppers

A savory “pantry-hero” recipe featuring Montreal-seasoned beef and a rich, doctored tomato glaze.

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color), halved and seeded
  • 1 lb (500g) ground beef
  • 2 tbsp Montreal steak spice
  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) Campbell’s tomato soup (undiluted)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (optional)

Instructions

  1. Soften the peppers.
    Preheat oven to 400°F. Place pepper halves open-side up in a 9×13-inch baking dish. Add a small splash of water to the bottom of the dish. Cover tightly with foil and bake for 15 minutes to steam until tender.
  2. Sear the beef.
    While peppers bake, brown ground beef in a skillet over medium-high heat. Season generously with Montreal steak spice as it cooks. Drain excess grease and transfer to a large mixing bowl.
  3. Create the “steakhouse” sauce.
    In a small bowl, whisk together tomato soup (do not add water), tomato paste, Worcestershire, onion powder, and Italian seasoning.
  4. Mix the filling.
    Add cooked rice and ¾ of the sauce to the bowl with beef. If using cheese, stir in half now. Mix until well-coated.
  5. Stuff and final bake.
    Remove peppers from oven and carefully drain any water from the pan. Spoon filling into each pepper. Top with remaining sauce and the rest of the cheese. Bake uncovered at 400°F for 20 minutes, until bubbly and golden.

Quick Tips

  • Salt warning: Montreal steak spice is salty—skip extra salt in the sauce/rice.
  • The rest: Let peppers sit 5 minutes after baking so the filling sets.
