Mexican Rice (Tomato Paste Method)

Toasted rice with fried tomato paste and boiling stock for fast, predictable, flavorful results.

← Back to Home
Prep: 5 min Cook: 25 min Total: 30 min Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 cups chicken stock (boiling)
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 cup of frozen vegetables

Instructions

  1. Prep stock: Bring the 2 cups of chicken stock to a boil and keep it hot.
  2. Toast rice: Heat oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add rice and stir frequently until golden brown, about 5–7 minutes.
  3. Fry paste: Add tomato paste to the rice. Stir for 1 minute until the paste darkens slightly and coats the grains.
  4. Season: Stir in onion powder, garlic powder, cumin, and salt.
  5. Combine: Add thawed frozen veggies. Carefully pour the boiling stock into the pan (it will steam vigorously).
  6. Simmer: Cover with a tight lid and reduce heat to low. Cook for 15 minutes.
  7. Rest: Turn off heat and let sit, covered and undisturbed, for 10 minutes.
  8. Finish: Fluff with a fork and serve.

Why this method is great

  • No heat lag: Adding boiling stock eliminates the 2–3 minutes it usually takes to come back up to temperature, so timings are more predictable.
  • Texture: The rice starts absorbing flavored liquid immediately for a more even cook from bottom to top.
