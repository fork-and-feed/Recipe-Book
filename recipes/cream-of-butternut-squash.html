Cream of Butternut Squash

Roasted squash + carrots blended silky with cream, butter, maple, and warm spices.

← Back to Home
Prep: 20 min Cook: 45 min Total: 65 min Serves: 6

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs butternut squash, peeled and cut into 1" cubes
  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups vegetable or chicken stock
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar (or dry white wine)
  • 4 tbsp butter (divided)
  • 4 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/8 tsp nutmeg
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Roast the Veggies
    Toss squash and carrots with a little oil and roast at 400°F for 30–35 minutes, until fork-tender with golden edges.
  2. Sauté the Base
    While squash roasts, melt 1 tbsp butter in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté onion and garlic until soft and translucent (about 5–8 minutes).
  3. Deglaze
    Pour in apple cider vinegar (or wine), scraping up browned bits. Simmer 1 minute.
  4. Combine & Blend
    Add roasted squash, carrots, and stock. Blend until completely smooth (immersion blender or in batches).
  5. Simmer
    Stir in cinnamon and nutmeg. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low.
  6. Finish
    Stir in heavy cream, maple syrup, and remaining 3 tbsp butter. Stir until melted and silky.
  7. Season
    Taste and add salt and pepper as needed before serving.

Notes

  • If you want it thinner, add a splash more stock.
  • For a little heat, add a pinch of cayenne or smoked paprika.
