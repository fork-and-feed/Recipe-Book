Cream of Broccoli (with Whole Florets)

Silky blended broccoli soup with tender whole florets folded in for texture.

← Back to Home
Prep: 15 min Cook: 25 min Total: 40 min Serves: 6–8

Ingredients

  • Broccoli: 1.5 kg (3–4 large heads)
  • Reserved florets: 2–3 cups small, bite-sized florets (set aside)
  • Potatoes: 5–6 large white potatoes (~1.5 kg), cubed
  • Onions: 2 medium onions, chopped
  • Garlic: 5 tbsp freshly chopped garlic
  • Chicken broth: 8–9 cups (~2 litres)
  • Heavy cream: 4 cups (1 litre)
  • Seasoning: 2 tbsp salt + 2 tbsp pepper (plus to taste)
  • Butter/oil: 3 tbsp

Instructions

  1. Sauté
    In a large stockpot, sauté onions and garlic in butter/oil until translucent.
  2. The “Texture” Step
    While onions sauté, bring a small pot of salted water to a boil. Add your reserved florets and blanch 3 minutes until tender-crisp and bright green. Drain and set aside.
  3. Simmer Base
    Add chicken broth, potatoes, and the bulk of the broccoli (stalks + remaining florets) to the stockpot. Cover and simmer 15–20 minutes until potatoes are very soft.
  4. Incorporate Cream
    Stir in heavy cream, salt, and pepper.
  5. Blend
    Remove from heat. Use an immersion blender to blend until completely smooth.
  6. Combine
    Fold the cooked whole florets back into the smooth soup. Serve immediately.

Notes

  • If it’s too thick, add a splash of broth to loosen.
  • Taste at the end—broth salt levels vary a lot.
