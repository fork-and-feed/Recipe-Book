Ground Beef Stir Fry (Onion & Pepper Version)

Fast 20-minute stir fry with a sweet-savory BBQ–soy glaze.

← Back to Home
Prep: 10 min Cook: 10 min Total: 20 min Serves: 4

Ingredients

The Beef & Veggies

  • 1 lb ground beef (80/20 or 90/10)
  • 1 large yellow onion, sliced into strips or wedges
  • 2 large bell peppers (any color), sliced into strips
  • 1 large carrot, julienned (matchsticks)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil or avocado oil

The Stir Fry Sauce

  • 3 tbsp BBQ sauce (original)
  • 3 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated

Instructions

  1. Whisk the Sauce
    In a small bowl, combine BBQ sauce, soy sauce, maple syrup, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger. Set aside.
  2. Brown the Beef
    Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat with 1 tbsp oil. Add ground beef and cook, breaking it up, until browned (about 5 minutes). Use a slotted spoon to remove beef and set aside, leaving drippings in the pan.
  3. Sauté the Onions & Peppers
    Add onion, bell peppers, and carrot to the same pan. Sauté 4–5 minutes, stirring frequently, until onions are translucent and peppers are crisp-tender.
  4. Combine
    Add cooked beef back into the pan with the vegetables.
  5. Thicken & Glaze
    Pour sauce over beef and vegetables. Toss 1–2 minutes until heated through and slightly thickened to glaze.
  6. Serve
    Serve immediately over white rice. Garnish with green onions or sesame seeds if desired.

Notes

  • If you want it spicier, add chili flakes or a squeeze of sriracha to the sauce.
  • For more sauce, double the sauce ingredients (great for extra rice).
