Rabbit Pie

A richer, more savory take on Richard’s classic.

← Back to Home
Prep: 30 min Slow cook: 4 hr Bake: 20–25 min Total: ~5 hr Serves: 6

Ingredients

The Filling

  • 1 rabbit, cleaned and jointed
  • 1 L chicken or beef broth
  • 1 cup dry red wine
  • 1 yellow onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 fresh rosemary branch
  • 1/3 cup rice (uncooked)
  • 1 cup frozen peas and corn mix
  • Salt, pepper, chili flakes, and a pinch of cayenne

The Sauce

  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 3 tbsp flour or cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste (deeper flavor than ketchup)
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 4 tbsp lemon juice

The Topping

  • 1 standard pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions

  1. Sear and Slow Cook
    Brown the Meat: Season rabbit pieces with salt and pepper. Sear in a little oil until golden on all sides. Move rabbit to the crockpot.

    Sauté Aromatics: In the same skillet, sauté onion and garlic 3–5 minutes. Add red wine to deglaze, scraping up browned bits.

    Start the Crockpot: Pour onion/wine mixture over the rabbit. Add broth, rosemary, chili flakes, and cayenne.
    Time: Cook on High 2 hours, then Low 2 hours.
  2. Prep the Components
    Cook rice separately according to package directions.
    Remove rabbit from crockpot, cool slightly, then pull meat from bones and shred. Discard bones and rosemary.
  3. Build the Savory Sauce
    Measure out 1 1/2 cups of cooking liquid from the crockpot.
    In a small saucepan, melt butter and whisk in flour (or starch) to make a paste.
    Slowly whisk in the cooking liquid, then whisk in tomato paste, Worcestershire, and lemon juice.
    Simmer on low until thick and glossy.
  4. Assembly and Bake
    Fold shredded rabbit, cooked rice, peas/corn, and thickened sauce together in a large bowl.
    Pour into a 9-inch pie dish (or 3 individual ramekins).
    Lay crust on top, trim edges, and cut a few slits for steam.
    Whisk egg with 1 tsp water and brush over crust.
    Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20–25 minutes, until puffed and golden.

Notes

  • Let the pie rest 10 minutes before slicing so the filling sets.
  • If your sauce thickens too much, whisk in a splash more cooking liquid or broth.
