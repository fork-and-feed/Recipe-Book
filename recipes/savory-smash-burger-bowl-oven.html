Savory Smash Burger Bowl (Oven Version)

Crispy roasted potatoes + smash-style beef + caramelized onions + burger sauce.

← Back to Home
Oven: 425°F / 220°C Total: ~45 min Serves: 3–4

Ingredients

Potatoes

  • 3–4 medium red potatoes, diced small
  • 1–2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp paprika (or smoked paprika)
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano or thyme
  • Optional: 1/4 tsp cayenne

Beef & Onions

  • 1 lb (450 g) ground beef
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/4 tsp mustard powder
  • Optional: pinch of MSG
  • Optional: a few drops Worcestershire sauce (at the end)

Toppings

  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • Optional: pickles, shredded lettuce

Burger Sauce

  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tbsp ketchup
  • 1 tbsp mustard
  • 1/2–1 tsp Cajun seasoning
  • Optional: 1–2 tsp pickle juice

Instructions

1) Roast the Potatoes

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). (Optional: preheat the sheet pan for better browning.)
  2. Toss potatoes with oil and all potato seasonings.
  3. Spread on a large baking sheet in a single layer (spacing matters).
  4. Roast 25–35 minutes, flipping halfway, until deep golden and crisp.

2) Caramelize the Onions

  1. Heat a little oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Cook onions 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until soft and deep golden brown. Set aside.

3) Cook the Beef (Smash Style)

  1. Increase heat to medium-high.
  2. Add ground beef, break it up, then press it into the pan.
  3. Let it sit briefly between stirs to build a crust. Cook 5–7 minutes until browned.
  4. Drain excess fat. Add beef seasonings and mix well.
  5. Optional: add a few drops of Worcestershire at the end.

4) Make the Sauce

  1. Mix mayo, ketchup, mustard, Cajun seasoning, and optional pickle juice. Adjust to taste.

5) Assemble

  1. Layer: crispy potatoes → hot beef → caramelized onions → cheddar (melts from heat) → sauce.
  2. Top with pickles and/or shredded lettuce if using.

Oven Notes

  • Key to crispiness: high heat + spacing + flipping.
  • If potatoes aren’t crisping, roast 5–10 minutes longer.
