General Thai–Style Popcorn Chicken

Wok-style crisp popcorn chicken tossed in a glossy sweet-tangy sauce with quick stir-fried veggies.

← Back to Home
Servings: 3–4 Total: ~25 min Method: Wok / Stovetop

Ingredients

Protein

  • 600–700 g frozen popcorn chicken (bake or air fry until deeply golden and crisp)

Sauce

  • 1/3 cup water
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 3 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp ketchup
  • 1 1/2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tsp vegetarian oyster sauce or pantry substitute (below)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp chili garlic sauce (optional)
  • Slurry: 1 1/2 tsp cornstarch + 1 tbsp cold water

Pantry Substitute for 1 tsp Oyster Sauce

If you don’t have vegetarian oyster sauce, mix and add directly into the sauce:

  • 3/4 tsp soy sauce
  • 1/4 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • Tiny pinch sugar

Vegetables

  • 1 cup red bell pepper, large chunks
  • 1/2–3/4 cup onion, large chunks
  • Optional: 2–3 dried red chilies
  • Green onions, sliced (to finish)

Method

  1. Cook the chicken: Bake or air fry popcorn chicken according to package instructions, cooking 1–2 minutes longer than directed for a firmer crust. Keep hot.
  2. Prepare sauce: Whisk all sauce ingredients together (including vegetarian oyster sauce or the pantry substitute). Keep the slurry separate for now.
  3. Stir-fry vegetables: Heat 1 tbsp neutral oil in a wok over high heat. Add onion and bell pepper (and dried chilies if using). Stir-fry 60–90 seconds — vegetables should remain slightly crisp.
  4. Thicken: Pour sauce into the wok and bring to a strong simmer. Stir the slurry, then add while stirring. Cook 30–45 seconds until glossy and medium-thick.
    Texture goal: coats the back of a spoon but still fluid.
  5. Finish: Lower heat. Add hot popcorn chicken and toss gently 20–30 seconds only. Remove immediately. Garnish with green onions and serve over jasmine rice.

Texture Control Notes

  • Do not simmer chicken in sauce.
  • Toss briefly to preserve the crisp coating.
  • Serve immediately for best texture.
