General Thai Chicken

Crispy fried chicken tossed in a glossy pineapple-soy chili sauce with quick stir-fried veggies.

← Back to Home
Prep: 15 min Cook: 15 min Total: 30 min Serves: 2–3

Ingredients

The Crispy Chicken

  • 1 lb chicken breast or thighs, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp white pepper
  • Oil for frying

The Signature Sauce

  • 1/2 cup pineapple juice
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp ketchup
  • 3 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 1/2 tsp red chili flakes
  • Slurry: 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 1 tbsp cold water

The Stir-Fry

  • 1/2 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1 large carrot, finely cubed

Instructions

  1. Coat the chicken: Toss chicken pieces in the beaten egg. In a separate bowl, mix cornstarch, flour, salt, and white pepper. Dredge the egg-washed chicken in the dry mix until heavily coated.
  2. Fry: Heat about 1 inch of oil in a heavy pan or wok to 350°F. Fry chicken in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 5–6 minutes. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  3. Make the sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together pineapple juice, soy sauce, ketchup, brown sugar, vinegar, garlic, ginger, and chili flakes. Set aside.
  4. Stir-fry veggies: Carefully pour off excess oil, leaving about 1 tbsp in the pan. On high heat, add the finely cubed carrots first for 60–90 seconds, then add onions and peppers for another 1 minute.
  5. Thicken: Pour the sauce into the pan with the veggies and bring to a simmer. Stir in the cornstarch slurry and cook about 1 minute until thick and glossy.
  6. The toss: Turn off heat. Add crispy chicken and toss quickly to coat.
  7. Serve: Serve immediately over jasmine rice or vermicelli noodles.

Notes

  • Keep the toss quick to preserve crispiness — sauce thickens fast.
  • For extra heat, add a dash of chili oil at the end.
