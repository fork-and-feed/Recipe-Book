Classic Shrimp Lo Mein

High-heat wok noodles with tender shrimp, glossy sauce, and crisp-tender vegetables.

← Back to Home
Serves: 2–3 Prep: 10 min Cook: 10 min Total: 20 min

Ingredients (2–3 servings)

Noodles + Oil

  • Lo mein noodles (225 grams, or enough for 2–3 servings)
  • 2–3 tbsp neutral oil

Shrimp Marinade

  • 1/2–1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp oil

Sauce

  • 2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp cooking wine (optional)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1/2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1/2 tsp chicken bouillon paste

Aromatics + Vegetables

  • 3–5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 3–4 scallions (separate whites and greens)
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 2–3 cups cabbage, sliced
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1 handful mushrooms, sliced
  • Bean sprouts (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prep shrimp & noodles: Pat shrimp dry and toss with light soy sauce, baking soda, cornstarch, and 1 tsp oil. Let sit while you prep everything else. Boil noodles until slightly underdone, drain, and set aside.
  2. Make sauce: Mix soy sauces, oyster sauce, cooking wine (optional), sesame oil, sugar, water, and cornstarch. Stir until smooth.
  3. Cook shrimp: Heat 1–2 tbsp oil over high heat. Sear shrimp 2–3 minutes until just cooked. Remove and set aside.
  4. Stir-fry vegetables: In the same pan, cook garlic and ginger briefly. Add scallion whites, carrots, cabbage, onion, and mushrooms. Stir-fry on high heat until slightly softened but still crisp.
  5. Combine: Add noodles and sauce and toss well. Return shrimp to the pan and mix. Cook 1–2 minutes until coated and glossy. Add scallion greens and bean sprouts at the end.

Notes

  • Don’t overcook shrimp — they cook fast and can turn rubbery.
  • Slightly undercook noodles so they don’t get mushy when stir-fried.
  • High heat is key for that classic lo mein texture and flavor.
