Biscuits

Flaky, buttery biscuits with simple layering folds.

← Back to Home
Yield: 8–10 Oven: 450°F Bake: 12–15 min

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (~250g)
  • 1 1/2 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 3/4 tsp salt
  • 113g unsalted butter (cold and cubed)
  • 210 ml whole milk (cold)
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter (melted and cooled)
  • Extra melted butter (for brushing)

Instructions

  1. Preparation
    Preheat oven to 450°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Mix Liquid
    Stir the 1 tbsp melted butter into the 210 ml cold milk. Keep in the fridge until needed.
  3. Combine Dry & Fat
    Whisk flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt. Cut the cold butter into the flour until it resembles small peas.
  4. Form Dough
    Stir in the cold milk mixture until just combined (don’t overmix).
  5. Fold
    Turn dough onto a floured surface. Fold it over itself 5–6 times to create layers.
  6. Cut
    Pat dough to 1-inch thick. Cut into circles by pressing straight down (do not twist).
  7. Bake
    Bake 12–15 minutes until golden. Brush with extra melted butter immediately.

Notes

  • Keep everything cold for maximum flake (cold butter + cold milk).
  • For taller biscuits, place them close together on the baking sheet.
