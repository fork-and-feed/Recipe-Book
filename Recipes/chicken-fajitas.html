Chicken Fajitas

Quick 30-minute fajitas with a flavorful homemade seasoning and juicy seared chicken.

← Back to Home
Prep: 15 min Cook: 15 min Total: 30 min Serves: 4

Ingredients

The Chicken & Marinade

  • Chicken: 2 large boneless skinless chicken breasts (~1.5 lbs)
  • Olive oil: 4 tbsp (divided)
  • Lime juice: 2 tbsp
  • Seasoning: 1 tsp each chili powder, cumin, paprika, onion powder, garlic powder, and fine sea salt; plus 1/2 tsp black pepper

The Veggies & Wraps

  • Bell peppers: 3 (red, yellow, green), sliced into 1/4" strips
  • Onion: 1 medium yellow onion, thinly sliced
  • Tortillas: 8 small flour tortillas

Optional Toppings

  • Lime wedges
  • Cilantro
  • Sour cream
  • Pico de gallo or salsa
  • Guacamole / avocado
  • Shredded cheese (Cheddar or Monterey Jack)

Instructions

  1. Prep the Chicken
    In a bowl, mix 2 tbsp oil, lime juice, and all spices. Slice chicken breasts in half lengthwise (to make thinner cutlets) and toss in the marinade. Let sit at least 15 minutes (or up to 4 hours).
  2. Prep Veggies
    Slice peppers and onion into 1/4" strips.
  3. Cook Chicken
    Heat a large heavy skillet (cast iron works great) over medium heat with 1 tbsp oil. Sear chicken 3–5 minutes per side until it reaches 165°F internal temp. Transfer to a cutting board and rest a few minutes.
  4. Sauté Veggies
    In the same skillet, add the final 1 tbsp oil. Sauté onions and peppers 5–6 minutes until softened and slightly browned. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Combine
    Slice chicken into strips against the grain and return to the skillet with the veggies. Toss to combine, then remove from heat.
  6. Serve
    Serve in warmed tortillas with your choice of toppings and a fresh squeeze of lime.

Tips for Success

  • Slicing: Slice onions and peppers with the grain so they hold their shape better while sautéing.
  • Heat the tortillas: Warm tortillas over a gas flame ~5 seconds per side for a little char.
  • Spiciness: This is fairly mild—add more chili powder or a pinch of crushed red pepper if you want heat.
