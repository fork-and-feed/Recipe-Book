Chicken Pot Pie Soup

All the cozy pot pie vibes — buttery, thick, and loaded with chicken and veggies.

← Back to Home
Prep: 15 min Cook: 30 min Total: 45 min Serves: 6

Ingredients

  • 8 tbsp (1 stick) unsalted butter
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 medium yellow onion, chopped
  • 2 medium carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 8 oz mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 lb Yukon Gold potatoes, sliced 1/4" thick
  • 5 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 6 cups chicken stock
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 cup frozen corn
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 3 tsp salt (plus more to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

Preparation

  1. Sauté
    Melt butter in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add onion, carrots, and celery and cook 5–7 minutes. Add mushrooms and garlic and sauté another 5 minutes.
  2. Thicken
    Stir in flour and cook 1 minute, stirring constantly to toast the flour.
  3. Simmer
    Gradually whisk in chicken stock. Add potatoes, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer (partially covered) 12–15 minutes until potatoes are tender.
  4. Finish
    Stir in chicken, peas, corn, and parsley.
  5. Creamy Step
    Pour in milk. Stir and heat until steaming, but do not let it reach a rolling boil. Taste and add more salt or pepper if needed.

Pro Tip

  • For an even thicker soup, smash a few cooked potato slices against the side of the pot before adding the milk. This releases natural starches that thicken the soup.
